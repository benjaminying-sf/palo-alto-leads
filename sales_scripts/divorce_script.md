# Divorce / Dissolution Call Script

**Use this when contacting:** The attorney(s) of record from a dissolution filing, or directly to one of the parties if you can identify them.

**Why they're motivated:** Courts frequently order the sale of the marital home as part of the property division. Both parties often want a fast, clean sale so they can finalize the settlement and move on with their lives. Off-market = faster close, no strangers walking through, less emotional friction.

**Who to call first:** The attorneys. They are the decision-influencers. Build relationships with family law attorneys and they will send you deals repeatedly.

**Note on leads:** Divorce court filings don't include property addresses. You'll need to cross-reference the parties' names with Santa Clara County Assessor records to find any Palo Alto property they own together.

---

## OPENER — CALLING THE ATTORNEY

> "Hi, is this [Attorney Name]? … Great. My name is [Your Name]. I'm a real estate investor based in Palo Alto. I specifically work with family law attorneys on dissolution cases involving real property, and I was hoping to introduce myself."

*(If gatekeeper/receptionist)*
> "Hi, my name is [Your Name] — I'm a real estate investor who works with dissolution cases. I'd like to speak with [Attorney Name] about a potential way to help clients who need to sell a marital property quickly. Is [he/she] available for a quick call, or may I schedule a time?"

---

## WHEN THE ATTORNEY ANSWERS

> "Thank you for taking my call — I'll be brief.
>
> My name is [Your Name] and I buy real estate for cash here in Palo Alto. I specifically focus on situations where clients going through a dissolution need to liquidate a property quickly — and I'm reaching out because I understand you filed a dissolution case [Case #] that may involve real property.
>
> Here's what I offer that's different from a traditional sale:
>
> - All-cash offer, usually within 24 hours of seeing the property
> - Close in 14–21 days, which keeps the settlement timeline on track
> - No open houses, no strangers in the home, fewer emotional flashpoints for both parties
> - I cover closing costs, so the net to the clients is cleaner to calculate for the split
>
> If either party has a Palo Alto property that needs to go, I'd love 10 minutes to show you how it works. This could be a real resource for your practice. Would you be open to a brief meeting or call?"

---

## IF ATTORNEY IS RECEPTIVE

> "Wonderful. I work with a lot of family law attorneys on a referral basis — if I close a deal with one of your clients, I'm happy to pay a referral fee through your firm in accordance with your bar rules, or simply make it easy for you to say you helped your client get a fast resolution.
>
> Can I send over a one-page overview of how I work and my past deal timeline? And if a case comes up where the parties need a quick sale, I'll do the heavy lifting — just send me the address and I'll handle everything from there."

---

## OPENER — CALLING A PARTY DIRECTLY

*(Use only if you've matched the court filing to a property owner via Assessor records)*

> "Hi, is this [Name]? … Hi, my name is [Your Name]. I'm a real estate investor in Palo Alto. I want to be respectful of your time and privacy — I'll be straightforward: I understand you may be going through a dissolution, and I work with people in that situation who need to sell a jointly-owned property quickly and privately.
>
> I'm not a realtor, there's no commission involved, and I can move much faster than a traditional listing. I know that getting the house sold is often one of the biggest things holding up a final settlement. Would you be open to a brief conversation?"

---

## IF THE PARTY IS INTERESTED

> "Great. A few quick questions:
>
> 1. Is both parties' agreement needed, or has the court already ordered a sale?
> 2. Is the property occupied right now, or vacant?
> 3. Roughly what's still owed on the mortgage?
>
> *(Listen)*
>
> Here's what I'd propose: I visit the property — I can be totally flexible on timing to accommodate both parties — and I'll give a written cash offer within 24 hours. If both parties agree, we close through a title company and the proceeds are disbursed according to your settlement agreement. The whole process typically takes 3 weeks or less.
>
> Does that sound like something worth exploring?"

---

## OBJECTIONS

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "My client needs court approval to sell." | "Understood — I'm familiar with that process and can structure the offer with appropriate contingency language. Having a written offer in hand can also actually speed up the court's approval since the terms are defined." |
| "We're going to list it with a realtor." | "That's a valid option. I'd just ask: in a traditional sale, you're looking at 30–90 days to close, multiple showings with both parties potentially needing to cooperate, and a ~2.5% commission off the top. I can close in 3 weeks with zero showings and zero commission, which makes the math on the net proceeds simpler for both parties." |
| "The other party won't cooperate." | "That's actually where I can sometimes help — my offer gives both parties a concrete number to react to, which often moves things forward when a stalemate has formed. Neither party is committing to anything by letting me see the property." |
| "We don't know what the property is worth." | "I can tell you within 24 hours. I'll run comps, assess the property, and give you a written number — at no cost and no obligation. You can use that as a data point." |

## BUILDING ATTORNEY RELATIONSHIPS

Family law attorneys are your highest-leverage contact for divorce leads. One good attorney relationship can send you 2–5 deals per year.

**Follow-up sequence after initial call:**
1. Send a one-page introduction email with your credentials and past deal timelines
2. Drop by their office with a handwritten note and business card
3. Connect on LinkedIn
4. Follow up quarterly — "Just checking in. Any dissolution cases with real property I might be able to help with?"

**What attorneys care about:**
- Speed: a fast close keeps their clients happy and ends the engagement sooner
- Reliability: they need to trust you won't back out at the last minute
- Simplicity: less paperwork, less drama for their clients

---

## NOTES

- **Best time to call attorneys:** 9–11am Tuesday–Thursday (avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons)
- **Most valuable call:** The introductory attorney relationship call, not the individual case call
- **Tax delinquent cross-tip:** Some divorce leads are *also* tax delinquent — check the report for any overlap
